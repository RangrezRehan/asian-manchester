## Asian Manchester – Catering Website

Asian Manchester is a delivery-only catering service based in Manchester, focused on small events of around 5–40 guests (with a core focus on 20–40 guests). This repository will hold the source code for the public-facing website that presents the business, menu, and pricing, and allows potential customers to make enquiries.

**Important**: No code has been developed yet. This README and `CONTEXT.md` define the business concept and website goals before implementation starts.

### Business Overview

- **Business name**: Asian Manchester  
- **Model**: Catering service only (no restaurant or takeaway)  
- **Service type**: Delivery catering  
- **Event size**: 5–40 guests (ideal range 20–40)  
- **Cuisine**: Indian and Pakistani cuisine with traditional desserts  
- **Service area**: Manchester initially, with potential expansion to nearby areas  

**Business statement**  
Asian Manchester provides authentic Indian and Pakistani catering for small events of 20–40 guests across Manchester and surrounding areas. The service specialises in freshly prepared curries, rice dishes, breads, and traditional desserts, delivered for private gatherings, birthdays, family celebrations, and community events.

The goal is to bring the rich flavours of South Asian cuisine to small events through:

- High-quality ingredients  
- Traditional recipes  
- Reliable and professional catering service  
- Clear pricing based on number of guests and menu selection  

### Menu Structure (Content Model)

The website will need to present the menu in clear categories, making it simple for visitors to understand options at a glance.

- **Mains**  
  - Examples: Nihari, Butter Chicken, Chicken Tikka Masala, Chicken Karahi (Peshawari Handi – with or without cream), Lamb Karahi (bone or boneless), Chicken Thigh Curry (red or green), Dal, Mixed Vegetable, Manchurian (Hot Garlic / Veg).

- **Rice dishes** (often the base of packages)  
  - Examples: Chicken Biryani, Kabuli Pulao, Lamb Leg Roast with Rose Bukhari Rice.

- **Sides** (bread and rice accompaniments)  
  - Examples: Naan, Roti, Boiled Rice, Pilau Rice, Fried Rice.

- **Starters**  
  - Examples: Pepper Winglets, Chaat, Paneer Tikka.

- **Soups** (optional add-ons, especially for winter events)  
  - Examples: Hot & Sour Soup, Lentil Soup, Manchow Soup.

- **Desserts / sweets**  
  - Examples: Gajrela with ice cream, Gulab Jamun with ice cream, Ras Malai with ice cream, Matanjan, Suji Shahi Halwa.

Later, the website content should make it easy to see which dishes are:

- Core (most popular and stable to offer)  
- Premium (special or higher-cost dishes)  
- Add-ons (extras like starters and soups)  

### Pricing Packages (Concept)

These packages define how pricing might be presented on the website. Exact prices and details can be adjusted later, but the structure is:

- **Basic Package**  
  - Chicken Biryani or Kabuli Pulao  
  - 1 curry  
  - Raita & salad  
  - Dessert  
  - Indicative price: **£12 per person**  

- **Standard Package**  
  - Rice dish  
  - 2 curries  
  - Naan  
  - Raita & salad  
  - Dessert  
  - Indicative price: **£15 per person**  

- **Premium Package**  
  - Premium rice dish  
  - 2 premium curries  
  - Starter  
  - Naan  
  - Dessert  
  - Indicative price: **£20 per person**  

The website should communicate that pricing is based on:

- Number of guests  
- Package level (Basic / Standard / Premium)  
- Choice of dishes within each category  

### Core, Premium, and Add-On Dishes

To help with both content and future implementation, dishes can be grouped by role:

- **Core dishes** (likely to be ordered most often)  
  - Mains: Butter Chicken, Chicken Karahi, Chicken Tikka Masala, Dal, Mixed Vegetable  
  - Rice: Chicken Biryani, Kabuli Pulao  
  - Desserts: Gulab Jamun, Ras Malai  

- **Premium dishes** (higher ingredient cost or effort)  
  - Nihari  
  - Lamb Karahi  
  - Lamb Leg Roast with Rose Bukhari Rice  
  - Matanjan  
  - Gajrela  

- **Optional / add-ons** (extras outside core packages)  
  - Starters: Pepper Winglets, Chaat, Paneer Tikka  
  - Soups: Hot & Sour Soup, Lentil Soup, Manchow Soup  

This grouping will be important later when designing package selection, highlighting upgrades, and managing content on the site.

### Website Goals (Phase 1)

In Phase 1, the website should focus on:

- Clearly explaining what Asian Manchester offers and which areas it serves.  
- Presenting the menu categories and example dishes in each.  
- Explaining Basic, Standard, and Premium packages in simple language.  
- Making it easy for visitors to understand how pricing works (per person, by package, adjusted by menu selection).  
- Providing a clear way for customers to enquire or request a quote (e.g., simple enquiry form or clear contact channels).  

No technical implementation decisions (frameworks, hosting, etc.) have been made yet and no code exists. Those will be defined in later steps.
